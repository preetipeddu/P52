Chameleons

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are a part of the iguana suborder.
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons.
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Bears

  • There are 8 species of bears found all around the world.
  • Bears typically live up to 25 years in the wild and 50 years in capacity.
  • Bears hibernate up to 100 days in the winter.

Camels

  • Camel's ears are furry.
  • Camels can move easily across the sand because of their specially-designed feet.
  • When they find water, they will drink as much as possible.

Pandas

  • Pandas spend a lot of their day eating.
  • Bamboo is a critical part of their diet.
  • A panda's eyes are different from normal bears.

Pigeons

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons can recognize each letter of the human alphabet.
  • Pigeons generally live for about 6 years.

Flying Cicadas

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • Cicadas are true bugs.
  • Their bodies serve as an important source of nitrogen.
